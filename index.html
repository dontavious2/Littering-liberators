For example, cigarette butts can contain chemicals such as arsenic and formaldehyde. These poisons can make their way into the soil and freshwater sources, which negatively impacts both humans and animals. In fact, 60% of water pollution is attributed to litter.

Animals are innocent victims affected by litter every day. Researchers estimate that over one million animals die each year after ingesting, or becoming entrapped in, improperly disposed trash. Plastic litter is the most common killer of animals and marine animals are the most notably affected. Each year over 100,000 dolphins, fish, whales, turtles and more drown after becoming entangled in or digesting plastic litter.

    About Us

    Our Team is on a vital mission to put an end to the blight of litter in our communities. With unwavering determination and a strong commitment to environmental preservation, their goal is to change behaviors and instill a sense of responsibility among individuals and communities. Through educational campaigns, community clean-up events, and advocacy for stricter littering laws, this dedicated group aims to raise awareness about the damaging effects of litter on our environment, wildlife, and public health. By encouraging responsible disposal of waste and fostering a culture of cleanliness, they aspire to create a cleaner, greener, and more sustainable future for all. Their mission is not just about picking up trash; it's about fostering a collective sense of stewardship for the planet we all share.
